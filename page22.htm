After the success of the mini-Queens, teenage Princesses were the obvious follow-up. This time it was a longer campaign, with 16 groups taking part. It was a very close contest. Panmure-East Coast won by £1,060 over South Auckland's £1,000. Third was North Shore. Mary Shelford was crowned in the Auckland Town Hall by Archbishop Liston. John Turei presented her with a greenstone mere and Mayor Robbie had plenty to say. The campaign lasted seven weeks. A total of £8,000 was raised by the Queen and the following 15 contestants:
